Priya

/ PREE-yah /
Core Meaning

Priya is a Sanskrit feminine name meaning "beloved," "dear," "darling," or "loved one." It expresses the essence of being cherished, treasured, and deeply loved.

Origin & Heritage

Priya originates from Sanskrit, one of the world's oldest languages, from the root word "priya" (प्रिय) meaning "dear" or "beloved." The name appears in ancient Vedic texts and has been used in the Indian subcontinent for thousands of years.

Religious Significance

In Hinduism, Priya is closely associated with devotion and love. It appears in sacred texts as an epithet for goddesses, particularly in the context of divine love and devotion. The concept of "priya" in Hindu philosophy represents the soul's beloved relationship with the divine.

Cultural Symbolism

Priya is one of the most popular names across India, Nepal, and among South Asian diaspora worldwide. It represents warmth, affection, and the cherished bond between people. The name is associated with positive personality traits including kindness, warmth, and a loving nature.
