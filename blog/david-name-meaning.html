Core Meaning

David is a Hebrew name meaning "beloved," "dear one," or "friend." It carries the meaning of being deeply cherished — one who is loved unconditionally by God and by people.

Origin & Heritage

David derives from the Hebrew name "Dawid" (דָּוִד) from the root "dwd" meaning "beloved" or "uncle." The name has been in continuous use for over 3,000 years, making it one of the oldest continuously used names in the world.

Religious Significance

David holds supreme importance in Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. In the Bible, King David was Israel's greatest king — a poet, warrior, and man after God's own heart who wrote most of the Psalms. In Islam, Dawud (David) is a revered prophet. The Star of David is the central symbol of Judaism.

Cultural Symbolism

David is one of the most universally recognized names across Western civilization. Michelangelo's statue of David is considered the greatest sculpture ever created. The name has been borne by kings, saints, artists, scientists, and leaders throughout history, symbolizing courage, creativity, and divine favor.
