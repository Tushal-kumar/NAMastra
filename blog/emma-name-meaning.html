Emma

/ EM-ah /
Core Meaning

Emma is a Germanic name meaning "whole," "universal," or "entire." It conveys completeness, strength of character, and a person who embodies wholeness in all aspects of life.

Origin & Heritage

Emma originates from the Old Germanic element "ermen" or "irmin" meaning "whole" or "universal." The name was introduced to England by the Normans and became extremely popular in medieval Europe. It experienced a massive revival in the 19th century and continues to be one of the most popular names worldwide.

Religious Significance

Emma has strong Christian roots in Europe, associated with several saints and queens who bore the name. Saint Emma of Gurk was a 11th-century Austrian noblewoman revered for her piety. The name has been borne by numerous European queens and noblewomen of Christian faith.

Cultural Symbolism

Emma is consistently ranked as one of the top names in the United States, United Kingdom, Australia, Canada, and throughout Europe. It gained literary fame through Jane Austen's novel "Emma" (1815). The name symbolizes grace, intelligence, and timeless elegance across Western cultures.
