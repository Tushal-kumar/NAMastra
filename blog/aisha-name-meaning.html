Aisha

/ AY-shah /
Core Meaning

Aisha is an Arabic feminine name meaning "alive," "living," or "she who lives." It conveys vitality, prosperity, and the blessing of a full and vibrant life.

Origin & Heritage

Aisha originates from classical Arabic, derived from the root word "asha" (عاش) meaning "to live." It has been in continuous use since the early Islamic period and remains one of the most beloved names across the Muslim world.

Religious Significance

In Islam, Aisha holds extraordinary significance as the name of the youngest and most beloved wife of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). She was renowned for her intelligence, scholarship, and deep knowledge of Islamic jurisprudence. She narrated thousands of hadiths and is considered one of the greatest Islamic scholars in history.

Cultural Symbolism

Aisha is deeply woven into the cultural fabric of Muslim societies worldwide. It is considered one of the most blessed names a girl can receive, symbolizing vitality, learning, and spiritual devotion. The name carries a sense of honor and dignity across Arab, South Asian, African, and Southeast Asian cultures.
