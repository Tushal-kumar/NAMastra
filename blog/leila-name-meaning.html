Leila

/ LAY-lah /
Core Meaning

Leila is an Arabic name meaning "night," "dark beauty," or "born at night." It evokes the mystery, romance, and serene beauty of the night sky.

Origin & Heritage

Leila derives from the Arabic word "layl" (ليل) meaning "night." The name has ancient roots in Arabic poetry and literature, where the night was celebrated as a time of beauty, mystery, and profound emotion.

Religious Significance

In Islamic tradition, the name Leila carries spiritual significance as the night is considered sacred — the Night of Power (Laylat al-Qadr) is one of the holiest nights in Islam. The name is widely used across Muslim communities and is considered beautiful and blessed.

Cultural Symbolism

Leila is immortalized in Persian and Arabic literature through the legendary love story of "Layla and Majnun," one of the most celebrated romantic tales in Eastern literature. The story has influenced poets from Rumi to Shakespeare. The name symbolizes unattainable beauty and deep passionate love.
