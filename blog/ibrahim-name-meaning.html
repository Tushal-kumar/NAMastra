Ibrahim

/ ib-rah-HEEM /
Core Meaning

Ibrahim is the Arabic form of Abraham, meaning "father of many nations" or "father of multitudes." It carries the profound meaning of leadership, spiritual fatherhood, and divine covenant.

Origin & Heritage

Ibrahim derives from the Hebrew name Abraham (אַבְרָהָם), composed of "av" meaning "father" and "hamon" meaning "multitude." The Arabic form Ibrahim has been used since the early Islamic period and is one of the most sacred names in Abrahamic traditions.

Religious Significance

Ibrahim holds supreme importance in Islam as one of the greatest prophets and the "Friend of God" (Khalilullah). He is considered the father of monotheism and is revered in Islam, Judaism, and Christianity. The Hajj pilgrimage rituals commemorate events from Ibrahim's life, making this name especially sacred.

Cultural Symbolism

Ibrahim is one of the most widely given names in the Muslim world, particularly in Arab, Turkish, Persian, and African Muslim communities. Naming a son Ibrahim is considered a great blessing and a declaration of faith. The name carries immense dignity, strength, and spiritual authority.
