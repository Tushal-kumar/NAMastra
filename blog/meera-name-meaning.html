Meera

/ MEE-rah /
Core Meaning

Meera is a Sanskrit name with multiple meanings — "ocean," "sea," "limit or boundary," and "prosperous." It also evokes light, brilliance, and the divine feminine energy.

Origin & Heritage

Meera derives from Sanskrit, with connections to the word "mira" (मीरा) which can mean "sea," "ocean," or "prosperous." The name gained eternal fame through the 16th-century Hindu saint-poet Mirabai, whose devotional songs to Lord Krishna remain among the most celebrated in Indian literature.

Religious Significance

In Hinduism, Meera is inseparably linked to Mirabai, the royal saint who devoted her entire life to Lord Krishna. Her bhajans (devotional songs) are considered sacred texts of devotion. The name symbolizes the highest form of bhakti (devotion) — surrendering completely to the divine with pure love.

Cultural Symbolism

Meera is deeply embedded in Indian cultural consciousness through the legendary story of Mirabai. The name represents unconditional love, artistic brilliance, and spiritual courage. Meera's story — a princess who chose divine love over earthly royalty — has inspired countless works of art, literature, and cinema.
